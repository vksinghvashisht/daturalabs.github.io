Questions What people ask most
The Maya Framework is my investment screening system built on the Sanskrit concept of maya — illusion. Most "opportunities" are pseudo-wealth: they look like value creation but are really complexity, fees, and institutional momentum dressed up as returns. The framework asks five questions of every deal: Is the capital requirement under $5M? Is the timeline to value realization under 18 months? What is the political risk? What is the regulatory complexity? What is the exit? If a deal can't answer all five cleanly, it's maya. I pass.
Because what Goldman sells is access to Goldman — not returns, not counsel, not alignment. The private banking relationship is designed around asset retention and cross-selling, not wealth creation. When I started building the infrastructure to evaluate deals myself, I realized I was paying eight figures in fees for a brand name and a Rolodex I didn't need. I write about this in detail. The short version: when you can do the work better yourself, you should.
Dharmic capitalism is not a philosophy — it is a system. Dharma means right action: action aligned with truth, with what is actually real, rather than what is fashionable. Applied to investing, it means screening for real value creation over optically attractive returns, maintaining integrity in deal structure, and building enterprises that compound over decades rather than sprinting to a liquidity event. Every framework I use — the Maya Framework, the triage pipeline, the deal screening criteria — is dharmic capitalism operationalized.

Datura Capital operates in three verticals: distressed commercial real estate (CMBS special servicing, loan-to-own, entitlement arbitrage), distressed oil and gas assets (363 bankruptcy sales, workover opportunities in basins with recoverable production), and fire zone lot arbitrage in Southern California (post-Palisades and Altadena). All deals must meet the Maya Framework criteria: under $5M capital deployment, under 18 months to value realization, and a clear exit. We do not do speculative land plays, early-stage technology investments, or deals where the thesis requires a market to move in our favor.
About Principal · Writer · Operator
VK Singh
Vashisht
Family Office Principal · Los Angeles

I run Datura Capital and Datura Labs out of the Arts District in downtown Los Angeles. Before this, I operated my family's natural gas vertical for twelve years — from 2010 through 2022 — in what most would describe as the worst environment for gas prices in a generation. That discipline is the foundation of everything I do now.

My investing thesis sits at the intersection of distressed assets, AI infrastructure, and the structural dislocations created when institutions move too slowly. I evaluate founders as whole people, not just pitch decks. I believe the wrapper problem is the defining failure mode of the current AI startup cycle. And I believe that human judgment, properly supported by AI, is an asymmetric advantage — not a limitation.

I write here because I am not on social media. If you want to know what I think, you come to my platform, engage with my work on my terms, and form your own view. That is the only arrangement that makes sense to me.
